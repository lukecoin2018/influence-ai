## What the Data Shows

Micro-influencers (50K–100K followers) deliver engagement rates 2.5× higher than top-tier accounts (300K–500K followers). Across every niche in our database, the pattern is consistent: as follower count grows, engagement rate falls.

But the more nuanced finding is this: **the relationship between follower count and engagement isn't linear — it drops sharply between 100K and 250K, then levels off.**

## The Full Data

| Follower Range | Label | Avg. Engagement Rate | Avg. Interactions Per Post |
|---|---|---|---|
| 50K–100K | Micro | 6.1% | 3,660 |
| 100K–200K | Mid-tier (lower) | 4.3% | 6,020 |
| 200K–300K | Mid-tier (upper) | 3.2% | 7,680 |
| 300K–500K | Top | 2.4% | 9,600 |

Notice the paradox: top-tier accounts (300K–500K) generate more total interactions per post despite lower engagement rates, simply because their audience is larger. The question isn't which tier has "better" engagement — it's which metric matters more for your campaign goal.

## Why Engagement Rate Falls as Follower Count Grows

### The audience composition shift
When an account has 80K followers, most of those followers discovered the creator organically because they genuinely liked their content. At 400K followers, a significant portion accumulated through platform recommendations, viral moments, collaborations, and follower-chasing behavior — not all of them deeply interested in the creator's specific niche.

### The community dynamic change
Smaller accounts feel like communities. Larger accounts feel like broadcasts. A creator with 90K followers often knows many of their regular commenters by name. A creator with 400K doesn't. This intimacy drives higher interaction rates at smaller scales.

### Algorithm reach differences
Instagram and TikTok algorithms don't show every post to every follower. As accounts grow, the percentage of followers reached per post tends to decrease. A post reaching 30% of 80K followers generates more relative engagement than one reaching 15% of 400K.

## Total Interactions: Where Size Wins

Despite lower engagement rates, larger accounts still produce more total interactions per post. For campaigns where raw interaction volume matters — comments for social proof, saves for algorithm boosting, shares for organic reach — top-tier creators have an advantage.

| Follower Range | Posts Needed to Reach 10,000 Interactions |
|---|---|
| 50K–100K (6.1%) | ~3 posts |
| 100K–200K (4.3%) | ~2 posts |
| 200K–300K (3.2%) | ~1.3 posts |
| 300K–500K (2.4%) | ~1 post |

For a brand running a single campaign post, top-tier creators deliver more interactions per post. For brands running sustained campaigns with multiple posts, micro creators can match or exceed total interaction volume at lower cost.

Micro-influencers deliver the best cost-per-interaction in most scenarios. But cost-per-interaction is only one metric — reach, brand safety, content quality, and audience fit all matter.

## When to Choose Each Tier

### Micro-influencers (50K–100K): Choose when...
- You want the highest engagement rate and best cost efficiency
- Your campaign needs authentic, niche-specific content
- You're running a test campaign to identify which creators perform best
- You're launching a new product and need early adopters with credibility
- Your budget is limited — run 5–10 micro campaigns for the cost of one top-tier partnership

### Mid-tier creators (100K–250K): Choose when...
- You need both meaningful reach and solid engagement
- You want a more established creator with proven content quality
- You're running a seasonal campaign that needs to reach a critical mass
- This is the tier that offers the best combination of reach and engagement — often the best default choice

### Top-tier creators (250K–500K): Choose when...
- Maximum reach per post is the priority
- You're running a brand awareness campaign where scale matters
- You need premium content production quality
- You're building brand credibility through association with well-known creators in a niche

## The Hybrid Approach

Many brands find the best results by combining tiers:

**Awareness + Conversion model:** Use 2–3 top-tier creators for broad awareness, then activate 8–12 micro creators to drive conversion through authentic recommendations. The top-tier content reaches a large audience; the micro creators close the deal.

**Test and scale model:** Run micro campaigns first to test messaging, products, and creator fit. Scale the formats and creator profiles that perform best with mid-tier or top-tier investment.
